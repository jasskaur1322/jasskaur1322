Introduction

What is Machine
Learning?

Machine Learning (ML) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence that enables computers to learn from data and improve their performance without being explicitly programmed.

Subset of AI

A specialized branch within the broader field of Artificial Intelligence.

Learns from Data

Absorbs knowledge from vast datasets and past experiences.

Identifies Patterns

Discovers hidden patterns and correlations within complex data.

Predictions & Decisions

Makes accurate predictions and intelligent decisions autonomously.

Categories

Types of Machine
Learning

Machine Learning is broadly classified into three fundamental types, each with its own approach to solving problems.

Type 01

Supervised Learning

Learns from labeled training data, making predictions based on known input-output pairs. Used in classification and regression tasks.

Classification Regression Labeled Data
Type 02

Unsupervised Learning

Works with unlabeled data to discover hidden patterns, groupings, and structures without predefined outcomes.

Clustering Association Unlabeled Data
Type 03

Reinforcement Learning

Learns through trial and error by interacting with an environment, receiving rewards or penalties for actions taken.

Reward System Agent Environment

Analysis

Pros & Cons

Advantages

Fast & Accurate Predictions

Processes massive datasets at incredible speeds with high precision.

Automates Repetitive Tasks

Frees up human resources by handling mundane, repetitive workflows.

Improves Decision-Making

Provides data-driven insights for better strategic decisions.

Learns from New Data

Continuously improves and adapts as more data becomes available.

Disadvantages

Requires Large Amount of Data

Needs massive, high-quality datasets to produce reliable results.

High Development Cost

Requires significant investment in infrastructure, tools, and talent.

Risk of Biased Results

Can inherit and amplify biases present in training data.

Privacy Concerns

Handling sensitive personal data raises serious privacy and ethical issues.
